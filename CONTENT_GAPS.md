# Content Gaps for a Fuller Picture of the Catholic Faith

This project already presents a strong foundation (core beliefs, sacraments, prayer, history, structure, apologetics, and resources). For a **more complete formation path**, the following additions would help.

## Highest-Impact Additions

1. **How to become Catholic (OCIA/RCIA pathway)**
   - Clear steps: inquiry, catechumenate, rites, sacraments at Easter, mystagogy.
   - Who should contact a parish and what to expect practically.

2. **Moral theology in daily life**
   - Formation of conscience, mortal vs. venial sin, near occasions of sin.
   - Practical examinations of conscience by vocation/state in life.

3. **Catholic Social Teaching**
   - Human dignity, common good, subsidiarity, solidarity, preferential option for the poor.
   - Concrete examples: work, migration, poverty, bioethics, civic responsibility.

4. **Discernment and vocation**
   - Discernment of marriage, priesthood, consecrated life, and single life.
   - Signs of a vocation and next steps.

5. **The liturgical year explained**
   - Advent, Christmas, Lent, Triduum, Easter, Ordinary Time.
   - Ember days, rogation traditions, and local/solemnity distinctions where relevant.

## Important Doctrinal/Formation Expansions

- **The Creed line-by-line** (Apostles' and Nicene Creed commentary).
- **The Ten Commandments and Beatitudes** as a moral framework.
- **Virtues and vices** (theological and cardinal virtues; seven deadly sins).
- **Grace and justification** with common misunderstandings clarified.
- **Ecclesiology depth**: marks of the Church, indefectibility, infallibility, development of doctrine.

## Scriptural and Catechetical Depth

- A **Bible roadmap** for Catholics:
  - Canon (including Deuterocanon), salvation history timeline, how to read Scripture with Tradition.
- A **Catechism guide**:
  - How CCC is organized (Creed, Sacraments, Moral life, Prayer).
  - Suggested reading tracks for beginners and returning Catholics.

## Prayer and Spiritual Life Gaps

- **Liturgy of the Hours** introduction and beginner plan.
- **Lectio Divina** step-by-step guide.
- **Fasting/abstinence discipline** by age, geography, and season with pastoral caveats.
- **Spiritual warfare basics** (balanced, Church-approved language).
- **Devotions by season** (Sacred Heart, Divine Mercy, Marian months, novenas).

## Pastoral/Practical Pages That Help Newcomers

- **How to attend Mass for the first time** (what to do, when to stand/kneel, Communion guidance).
- **How to go to confession** (short guide + full examination + act of contrition options).
- **Marriage and family life**
  - Openness to life, NFP overview, parenting in the faith.
- **Suffering, mental health, and accompaniment**
  - Encouragement to seek professional help alongside spiritual support.

## Apologetics Expansion Opportunities

- Papacy and apostolic succession (historical argumentation depth).
- Marian doctrines (Immaculate Conception, Assumption) with scriptural typology.
- Justification and sanctification with Protestant/Catholic distinctions.
- Problem of evil and scandal in the Church (clear, honest treatment).

## Suggested Information Architecture Upgrade

Add a "Start Here" sequence for progressive formation:

1. **Gospel + basic kerygma**
2. **Creed essentials**
3. **Sacraments and Sunday Mass**
4. **Prayer habit plan (daily/weekly)**
5. **Moral life and confession**
6. **Vocation and mission**

This keeps visitors from feeling overwhelmed and makes the site useful to both inquirers and practicing Catholics.
